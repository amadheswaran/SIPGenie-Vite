SIP vs Fixed Deposit (FD): Which is Better for You?

FD gives stability; SIP offers growth. Here’s a numbers-first comparison across returns, risk, liquidity and tax, so you can pick confidently.

Quick head-to-head

FactorFDSIP (Equity MFs)
Returns potentialFixed (bank rate)Market-linked, higher long-term potential
RiskLow (bank/insured*Market volatility, reduces with time in market
LiquidityPremature withdrawal penaltyRedeem any time (T+2 or faster)
TaxInterest taxed as incomeLTCG @10% above ₹1L gains, STCG @15%
Inflation protectionPoorBetter over long horizons

When FD makes sense

  • Emergency buffer or money needed within 1–2 years.
  • Zero tolerance for volatility.

When SIP wins

  • Goals 5+ years away (education, retirement).
  • Comfortable with short-term ups/downs for higher long-term growth.
Pro tip: Combine both. Park 6–12 months expenses in FD/LIQ debt, invest the rest via SIP (with a 5–10% step-up) toward goals.
